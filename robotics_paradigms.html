Robotics Paradigms (Without HCI)

Robotics paradigms describe the main approaches to robot design and control, focusing on how robots sense, decide, and act. Here are the three classic paradigms:
  • Sense-Plan-Act (SPA) Paradigm: The robot perceives its environment, creates a plan, then acts. Good for structured, step-by-step tasks.
  • Reactive Paradigm: The robot reacts instantly to sensor inputs with predefined actions, without internal planning. Useful for fast, simple responses.
  • Hybrid Paradigm: The robot combines both reactive behaviors and planning. Balances quick responses with intelligent decision-making.
Paradigm Key Idea Example
Sense-Plan-Act Perceive → Plan → Act Path planning in a warehouse
Reactive Immediate response to sensor data Obstacle avoidance on a rover
Hybrid Mix of planning and reacting Search-and-rescue robots
